Preservation

Preservation isn’t easy. It requires meticulous attention to detail. It requires difficult-to-find tools and equipment. It requires skilled hands that understand the object’s physical makeup, how it deteriorates, and what must be done to stabilize it.

And yet, it is important work. The physical object continues to be essential for research and teaching for everything from molecular analysis of vellum manuscripts, to reproduction of the home movie experience with Kodachrome’s vibrant color film, to more traditional studies of the technology of the book. Maps and architectural drawings determine ownership of land and document building construction. Audio and moving images provide a unique and powerful tool with which to study the past, present, and future. Preservation Services supports the teaching and research mission of the University of Virginia by facilitating access to the Library’s vast research collections—regardless of format.

PRESERVING CIRCULATING COLLECTIONS

Preservation Services staff assess incoming books for treatment versus replacement options in order to make sure that the selected treatment is the best value for the Library. Books formerly placed in boxes are now being repaired using more complex techniques, providing a more usable book for the U.Va. research community.

PRESERVING SPECIAL COLLECTIONS

The book and paper conservation lab is a full service facility allowing the Library to provide conservation treatment for Special Collections materials that formerly had to be outsourced. Books, maps, manuscripts, and drawings can be washed, mended, alkalized, and re-bound according to their needs.

PRESERVING AUDIOVISUAL COLLECTIONS

The Library has dramatically increased its ability to care for our valuable but fragile audiovisual materials. Preservation Services has built inspection and viewing stations for film, and reformatting stations for audio and video. We now have the ability to clean, repair, and reformat audiocassettes, reel-to-reel, most grooved media, and VHS, S-VHS, Umatic and Beta tapes.

THE ANDREW W. MELLON FOUNDATION CHALLENGE GRANT

The Preservation Labs were equipped with funding from a generous award from The Andrew W. Mellon Foundation. This grant has provided the U.Va. Library with a new foundation upon which to grow the Preservation Services program. The Library needs to secure significant support from donors in order to receive additional matching funds.

With this additional support, Preservation Services will be able to expand the scope of its services to meet the challenges of audiovisual and digital preservation while continuing to preserve the physical collections vital to a top research institution. Interested in learning more about how you can help Preservation Services meet the challenge? Contact Major Gifts Officer Patrick Garcia at 434-924-9640 or pmg3x@virginia.edu.
